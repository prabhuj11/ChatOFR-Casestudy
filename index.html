01 · The Problem

A 45-Day Onboarding
Process No One Could Fix

JADE — the Joint Analysis Data Environment — was a secure analytics platform connecting five federal regulatory agencies. But onboarding a new researcher was a fragmented, manual nightmare.

Across two disconnected systems with no automation

04

After 45 days of back-and-forth, the researcher finally accesses data. Momentum lost. Trust eroded.
02 · Discovery

Seeing the Opportunity
Before Anyone Else Did

OFR was deploying an internal chatbot using Claude Sonnet and Haiku to help staff navigate distributed SharePoint sites across Treasury. It was purely internal — nobody had thought about applying it externally.

The Pitch
"What if we adapt this chatbot architecture to automate the JADE researcher onboarding workflow? Instead of asking researchers to navigate ServiceNow and Collibra manually, the AI handles the routing, validation, and provisioning — automatically."
Why it was feasible
  • ✓ Claude Sonnet already deployed inside Treasury
  • ✓ ServiceNow and Collibra both had APIs
  • ✓ Onboarding workflows were rule-based and deterministic
  • ✓ Small team, fast iteration possible
Why it was risky
  • △ Federal regulatory environment — failure is visible
  • △ Five agencies, five different entitlement models
  • △ AI hallucination risk in access provisioning
  • △ No prior AI-automated access model at OFR
✓
Leadership heard the pitch and approved moving forward — with the condition that we prove it technically before touching any external agency.

04 · Roadmap

Sequenced for
Risk, Not Speed

The roadmap wasn't built around shipping fast. It was built around proving each assumption before the next one depended on it.

Phase 0 · Foundation
Internal Chatbot Deployed by OFR
Claude Sonnet + Haiku deployed for internal Treasury staff navigation across SharePoint sites. Proof that the model works in a federal security environment.
Phase 1 · Technical Proof
ChatOFR Concept Approved — ServiceNow Pilot 1
Pitched the external onboarding use case. Approved. Tested one ServiceNow workflow integration to prove technical feasibility before investing further.
Phase 2 · Integration Build
Full ServiceNow + Collibra Integration
Expanded ServiceNow to all workflows incrementally. Then piloted one Collibra dataset request. Then added all remaining datasets. Full integration achieved before any external user saw the product.
Phase 3 · External Pilot
FDIC Pilot — First External Agency
FDIC researchers used ChatOFR for real onboarding requests. Collected structured feedback. Fixed undiscovered issues. Validated the 12-day onboarding outcome.
Phase 4 · Launch
Full Rollout — FRB, OCC, SEC
FDIC became an internal evangelist. Announced ChatOFR to all remaining agencies with FDIC's endorsement. Smooth adoption — agencies arrived with peer validation, not skepticism.
05 · Pilot Strategy

Six Nested Pilots.
One Layer at a Time.

Rather than running a single big pilot, I ran six nested proof points — each one de-risking a specific assumption before the next layer was added. By the time FDIC saw ChatOFR, every technical and data governance question had already been answered.

06 · The Challenge

Leadership Wanted
to Launch Now. I Disagreed.

After the ServiceNow integration was complete, leadership saw working technology and wanted to do an immediate big-bang rollout to all five agencies. I pushed back — and made the case for the phased approach.

Leadership's Position
"We have working technology. Let's launch to all agencies now."
The ServiceNow integration worked. Leadership wanted to show impact quickly. Waiting felt like unnecessary delay when the product was functional.
My Position
"We've proven one layer. We haven't proven the product."
ServiceNow was working but Collibra wasn't integrated yet. We had no external user validation. A failed big-bang rollout in a federal regulatory environment damages trust in AI across the entire ecosystem — trust that takes years to rebuild.
✓

Leadership agreed — immediately

I made a reasoned case: the fastest path to full adoption in a trust-sensitive environment is often a slower, more deliberate one. Leadership heard it and changed course. The FDIC pilot discovered issues we hadn't anticipated internally. We fixed them before anyone else saw them. The full rollout was smoother because of the pilot — not despite it.

"In a federal regulatory environment, a failed public rollout doesn't just set back one product — it sets back trust in AI across the entire agency ecosystem. The nested pilot approach meant every layer was proven before the next one was added."

— Lesson from the ChatOFR rollout

08 · Post-Launch Reflection

What I'd Do
Differently. What I'd Keep.

The product shipped and achieved its goals. But honest post-launch reflection reveals what I'd change — and what principles I'd carry forward into every AI product I build.

△ What I'd do differently

  • Involve the compliance and legal teams earlier in the intent classification design — we discovered edge cases during the FDIC pilot that a compliance review would have caught sooner.
  • Build the audit trail and logging framework before writing the first workflow, not after. Governance is a design constraint, not an afterthought.
  • Define the exception handling model in writing before engineering started building — the rules for when the AI escalates to a human were clarified too late in the process.

✓ What I'd keep

  • The nested pilot strategy. Every layer proven before the next one depended on it. Non-negotiable for AI products in regulated environments.
  • Recruiting a pilot agency before a full rollout — and choosing an agency that would give honest feedback, not just compliance.
  • Treating FDIC as a design partner during the pilot rather than a test subject. Their feedback shaped the product, not just validated it.
  • Starting with one workflow, not all of them. The instinct to prove before you scale is right every time.
"Government clients need to trust what they're adopting before they'll evangelize it to others. The fastest path to full adoption is often a slower, more deliberate one."
